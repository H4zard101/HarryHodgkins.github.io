What is a gameplay programmer?

A gameplay programmer is someone that primarily codes the code base for games. They work with other teams like level designers etc to figure out what needs to be done and the best way to approach and work on it. Because they work with different teams within the development team you could make the argument that they have many different specialisations that all could fall under the term of a gameplay programmer.

What does the role intel?

As mentioned previously the role requires them to be able to work collaboratively, they work with different teams to get the idea of what they need to do, and the gameplay programmers are the ones that get it done. They also need to communicate. This goes hand in hand with the collaboration that they do with other teams.

Salary

There are various salaries they you could be paid according to glassdoor the average base pay for a gameplay programmer is £31,000 but, the low to high pay rates range from £25,000 to up to £49,000. You could debate this figure however as glassdoor calculates these figures based on anonymous submitted reviews.

Advice for aspiring gameplay programmers

Be passionate about what you do, always look to learn, and improve. I would also advise people to browse different games companies and look at things they expect from you and to if possible, get into contact with some people in the industry and ask them. Ask what they look for, what they wish they would have known when learning in this particular job and what advice they would give to someone looking to get started as a gameplay programmer.
